# SCQA Memo

Apply the SCQA framework to transform messages or documents into structured executive memos with clear narrative flow.

## The Methodology

**Source:** McKinsey & Company / Barbara Minto, _The Pyramid Principle_

SCQA creates a narrative arc that guides the reader from shared context to your recommendation:

1. **Situation** — Establish common ground. What is the current state that everyone agrees on? This anchors the reader in familiar territory.

2. **Complication** — Introduce tension. What has changed, gone wrong, or created a problem? This is why the reader should care.

3. **Question** — The question the complication raises. What must be resolved? This focuses attention on what matters.

4. **Answer** — Your recommendation or conclusion. This resolves the tension and provides the path forward.

### Why This Order Matters

- **Situation** builds agreement before introducing conflict
- **Complication** creates the "so what" — why this memo exists
- **Question** focuses the reader on the right problem
- **Answer** lands with impact because the reader is primed

### Common Variations

| Variant                  | Use When                                  |
| ------------------------ | ----------------------------------------- |
| **SCA** (skip Question)  | Question is obvious from the Complication |
| **CQA** (skip Situation) | Situation is well-known to audience       |
| **Full SCQA**            | New audience or complex/sensitive topic   |

## Process

1. **Extract the recommendation**
   - Find the answer or proposal in the source material
   - If unclear, ask: "What action or decision does this support?"

2. **Identify the complication**
   - What problem, change, or tension drives the need?
   - Look for: "The issue is...", "However...", "The challenge..."
   - This is usually why the memo exists

3. **Establish the situation**
   - What context does the reader need?
   - What facts are uncontroversial and shared?
   - Keep minimal — only what's needed to understand the complication

4. **Frame the question**
   - What question does the complication naturally raise?
   - This should lead directly to your answer
   - If the question doesn't lead to your answer, revisit the complication

5. **Structure the memo**
   - Lead with SCQA in that order
   - Supporting details follow the Answer
   - Group supporting points logically (MECE where possible)

6. **Validate the flow**
   - Read S then C — does tension emerge naturally?
   - Read C then Q — is the question obvious?
   - Read Q then A — does the answer resolve it?

## Example

### Before (Unstructured)

```
I wanted to share some thoughts on our customer support situation. We've been
getting more tickets lately and the team is struggling. Response times are up
to 48 hours now. I looked at the data and it seems like the new product launch
brought in a lot of users who need more hand-holding. The team has asked for
help but we're in a hiring freeze. I think we should consider a few options:
maybe a better FAQ, or some automation, or temporary contractors. We need to
decide soon because the team is burning out.
```

### After (SCQA Structured)

```
## Customer Support Response Times

**Situation:** Our support team has successfully handled growth to date,
maintaining 24-hour response times through Q2.

**Complication:** Since the July product launch, ticket volume has increased
40% while response times have degraded to 48 hours. The new user cohort
requires more guidance, and the hiring freeze prevents adding headcount.
The team is showing signs of burnout.

**Question:** How do we restore acceptable response times without adding
permanent headcount?

**Answer:** Implement a three-part solution:

1. **Self-service (immediate)** — Expand FAQ with top 10 new-user questions
2. **Automation (2 weeks)** — Deploy auto-responses for common ticket types
3. **Temporary capacity (if needed)** — Engage contractors for 60-day surge

Expected outcome: Return to 24-hour response times within 30 days.

**Decision needed:** Approve contractor budget ($X) as contingency by Friday.
```

## Quality Checks

- [ ] Situation is factual and uncontroversial — reader nods along
- [ ] Complication creates genuine tension — reader feels the problem
- [ ] Question flows naturally from complication — reader thinks "yes, that's the question"
- [ ] Answer directly resolves the question — no mismatch
- [ ] S → C → Q → A reads as a coherent narrative
- [ ] Supporting details appear after the Answer, not before
- [ ] Memo is shorter or equal length to original
- [ ] Clear action or decision requested (if applicable)
