# BLUF

Restructure communications using the Bottom Line Up Front method.

## The Methodology

**Source:** U.S. Military communication doctrine

The BLUF format prioritizes reader efficiency by inverting typical narrative structure:

1. **Bottom Line** — State conclusion or request first
2. **Background** — Minimal context the reader needs
3. **Details** — Supporting information (only if required)
4. **Action** — Explicit statement of what you need

## Process

1. **Extract the bottom line**
   - Find the conclusion, decision, or request
   - This is often buried in the middle or end
   - Ask: "If the reader stops after one sentence, what must they know?"

2. **Lead with it**
   - Make it the first sentence
   - No preamble, no context-setting first

3. **Add minimal background**
   - Only what's required to understand the bottom line
   - If they could act without this context, cut it

4. **Include details sparingly**
   - Supporting evidence, data, or reasoning
   - Only if the reader needs it to make a decision

5. **End with explicit action**
   - What do you need from the reader?
   - By when?

6. **Cut aggressively**
   - Remove qualifications that don't change meaning
   - Delete repetition
   - Eliminate throat-clearing phrases

## Example

**Before:**

> Hi team, I wanted to follow up on our discussion from last week's planning meeting. As you may recall, we talked about the Q3 roadmap and some of the challenges we're facing with the current timeline. After reviewing the latest sprint velocity data and talking with the engineering leads, I've been thinking about our options. Given the complexity of the authentication refactor and the upcoming holiday schedule, I think we should consider pushing the launch date. Specifically, I'm proposing we move from September 15th to October 1st. This would give us a two-week buffer that I believe we need. Let me know your thoughts when you get a chance.

**After:**

> I recommend moving the launch from September 15th to October 1st. Please confirm by Friday.
>
> Why: Sprint velocity data and engineering feedback indicate we need a two-week buffer, primarily due to authentication refactor complexity and the upcoming holiday schedule.

## Quality Checks

- [ ] First sentence contains the main point or request
- [ ] Reader knows what's needed within 5 seconds
- [ ] No "burying the lead" — conclusion isn't hidden
- [ ] Action required is explicit (what and when)
- [ ] Background is minimal — only what's necessary
- [ ] No throat-clearing ("I wanted to...", "As you know...")
