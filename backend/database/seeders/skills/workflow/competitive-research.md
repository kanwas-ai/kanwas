# Competitive Research

Research competitors systematically and synthesize findings into actionable insights.

## What This Orchestrates

1. Define research scope and decision context
2. Identify and categorize competitors
3. Gather information per competitor
4. Structure comparison by relevant dimensions
5. Extract insights and opportunities
6. Synthesize recommendations

## Process

### 1. Clarify Research Goal

Before researching, establish:

- **Decision context**: What decision will this research inform?
- **Scope**: Quick scan (30 min) or deep dive (2+ hours)?
- **Focus**: Features? Positioning? Pricing? Go-to-market?

Ask if unclear: "What decision are you trying to make with this research?"

### 2. Identify Competitors

Categorize into three buckets:

| Category         | Definition                           | Example                                |
| ---------------- | ------------------------------------ | -------------------------------------- |
| **Direct**       | Same problem, same solution approach | Figma vs Sketch                        |
| **Indirect**     | Same problem, different approach     | Figma vs PowerPoint                    |
| **Aspirational** | Different space, relevant model      | Figma learning from Notion's community |

Start with 3-5 competitors. Expand if needed.

### 3. Gather Information

For each competitor, collect:

**Core positioning**

- Who they serve (target segment)
- What problem they solve (value prop)
- How they're different (positioning)

**Product specifics** (based on focus area)

- Features and capabilities
- Pricing model and tiers
- Distribution channels
- Recent changes or announcements

**Market signals**

- Funding, growth, headcount trends
- Customer sentiment (reviews, social)
- Strategic moves (acquisitions, pivots)

Use web search for current information. Note information age and reliability.

### 4. Structure Comparison

Choose structure based on research goal:

**Feature comparison** (for product decisions)

```
| Feature | Us | Comp A | Comp B | Comp C |
|---------|-----|--------|--------|--------|
| [Feature 1] | Yes | Yes | No | Partial |
```

**Positioning map** (for strategy decisions)

- Plot competitors on 2 relevant dimensions
- Example axes: Enterprise vs SMB, All-in-one vs Point solution

**Pricing comparison** (for monetization decisions)

```
| Tier | Us | Comp A | Comp B |
|------|-----|--------|--------|
| Free | [limits] | [limits] | [limits] |
| Pro | $X/mo | $Y/mo | $Z/mo |
```

### 5. Extract Insights

Go beyond facts. Identify:

**Gaps**: What do competitors miss that customers want?
**Threats**: Where are competitors stronger or moving fast?
**Opportunities**: Where can we differentiate or leapfrog?
**Patterns**: What trends appear across the landscape?

Frame insights as "So what?" statements:

- Not: "Competitor X has feature Y"
- Instead: "Competitor X's feature Y is gaining traction with [segment], suggesting we should [action]"

### 6. Synthesize Recommendations

Tie findings to the original decision context:

- 2-3 key takeaways (what matters most)
- Specific recommendations (what to do)
- Open questions (what needs more research)

## Adapts To

**Research depth**

- Quick scan: Focus on positioning and one comparison dimension
- Standard: Full process with 3-5 competitors
- Deep dive: Expand competitors, add customer research, multiple frameworks

**Focus area**

- Feature focus: Heavy on capability comparison, light on positioning
- Strategy focus: Heavy on positioning maps, market signals
- Pricing focus: Detailed tier analysis, value metrics

**Available information**

- Public companies: More data available (financials, analyst reports)
- Startups: Rely more on product teardowns, customer reviews
- Pre-launch: Focus on team backgrounds, investor signals

**Stakeholder audience**

- Executive: Lead with insights and recommendations
- Product team: Lead with feature comparison
- Sales team: Lead with competitive positioning and objection handling

## Quality Checks

- [ ] Research goal is explicit — a specific decision is identified
- [ ] Competitor categories are justified — why these competitors?
- [ ] Comparison structure matches the decision being made
- [ ] Insights are actionable — "so what" is clear for each finding
- [ ] Recommendations tie back to original context
- [ ] Sources are cited and information freshness is noted
- [ ] Gaps in information are acknowledged, not glossed over
