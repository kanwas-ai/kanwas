# Stakeholder Mapping

Build a clear picture of who matters for your initiative, where they stand, and how to engage them.

## What This Orchestrates

1. Project context gathering
2. Stakeholder identification and categorization
3. Influence/interest matrix construction
4. Engagement strategy development

## Process

### 1. Establish Project Context

Before mapping stakeholders, understand what you're mapping them for.

**Gather:**

- What is the initiative? (scope, goals, timeline)
- What decisions need stakeholder input?
- What resources or approvals are required?
- What changes will this initiative create?

**If context is unclear, ask:**

- "What are you trying to accomplish?"
- "What could go wrong if key people aren't on board?"

### 2. Identify Stakeholders

Cast a wide net first, then categorize.

**Discovery questions:**

- Who has decision authority over this?
- Who controls resources you need (budget, people, systems)?
- Who will be directly affected by the outcome?
- Who has expertise or information you need?
- Who has blocked similar initiatives before?
- Who are the informal influencers?

**Categories:**

| Category         | Description                                 |
| ---------------- | ------------------------------------------- |
| Decision-makers  | Can approve, veto, or fund                  |
| Influencers      | Shape opinions of decision-makers           |
| Contributors     | Provide work, expertise, or resources       |
| Affected parties | Impacted by outcomes, may resist or support |

**Watch for gaps:**

- End users often forgotten in favor of executives
- Support/ops teams often overlooked
- External stakeholders (customers, partners, regulators)

### 3. Build the Influence/Interest Matrix

For each stakeholder, assess two dimensions:

**Influence:** Ability to affect outcomes

- Decision authority (formal power)
- Resource control (budget, people, access)
- Political capital (relationships, credibility)
- Expertise that others defer to

**Interest:** Degree of concern about the initiative

- Direct impact on their work or goals
- Alignment with their priorities
- Past involvement in similar initiatives
- Stake in the outcome (positive or negative)

**Matrix placement:**

```
              HIGH INFLUENCE
                    |
     KEEP           |        MANAGE
     SATISFIED      |        CLOSELY
                    |
                    |
LOW INTEREST -------|------- HIGH INTEREST
                    |
                    |
     MONITOR        |        KEEP
                    |        INFORMED
                    |
              LOW INFLUENCE
```

**Quadrant strategies:**

| Quadrant       | Strategy                                                                                                  |
| -------------- | --------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------- |
| Manage Closely | High influence + high interest. Your key players. Regular engagement, involve in decisions.               |
| Keep Satisfied | High influence + low interest. Can block you if unhappy. Keep informed, don't overwhelm.                  |
| Keep Informed  | Low influence + high interest. Care deeply but limited power. Good feedback sources, potential advocates. |
| Monitor        | Low influence + low interest. Minimal effort. Check periodically for changes.                             |

### 4. Assess Current Positions

For stakeholders in "Manage Closely" and "Keep Satisfied" quadrants:

| Position  | Signals              | Implication                    |
| --------- | -------------------- | ------------------------------ |
| Champion  | Actively advocating  | Leverage, protect relationship |
| Supporter | Positive but passive | Activate when needed           |
| Neutral   | No clear stance      | Educate, build relationship    |
| Skeptic   | Has concerns         | Understand objections, address |
| Opponent  | Actively resisting   | Neutralize or work around      |

**Position is not fixed.** People move based on new information, changing context, or how you engage them.

### 5. Develop Engagement Plan

For each key stakeholder (top two quadrants), define:

| Stakeholder | Position  | Goal     | Approach        | Frequency   |
| ----------- | --------- | -------- | --------------- | ----------- |
| [Name/Role] | [Current] | [Target] | [How to engage] | [How often] |

**Engagement approaches by position:**

- **Champions:** Keep close, use as advocates, share credit
- **Supporters:** Activate for key moments, keep informed
- **Neutrals:** Build relationship, understand their world, find shared interests
- **Skeptics:** Listen first, address concerns directly, find common ground
- **Opponents:** Understand root cause, negotiate if possible, contain if not

**Decision point:** If a critical stakeholder is opposed and won't move:

- Can you proceed without them?
- Is there a path around their objection?
- Does this require escalation?

## Adapts To

**Organizational complexity**

- Small team/startup: Focus on 3-5 key people, informal mapping
- Large org/cross-functional: Formal matrix, explicit engagement cadence
- External stakeholders involved: Add regulatory, partner, customer perspectives

**Initiative scope**

- Tactical (small change): Quick assessment, focus on direct decision-makers
- Strategic (major initiative): Full mapping, coalition building, executive sponsors
- Transformational (org change): Multiple waves, ongoing reassessment

**Political sensitivity**

- Low stakes: Transparent mapping, share openly
- High stakes: Keep mapping confidential, careful about what you reveal

**Information availability**

- New to org: Lean on others' knowledge, explicitly ask about dynamics
- Established relationships: Leverage existing knowledge, validate assumptions

## Quality Checks

- [ ] All four quadrants have been considered, not just obvious stakeholders
- [ ] Influence and interest assessments are based on evidence, not assumptions
- [ ] Key stakeholders have engagement approaches tailored to their position
- [ ] Relationships between stakeholders are noted (who influences whom)
- [ ] The map reflects who can actually block or enable progress
- [ ] Engagement plan is actionable, not generic
- [ ] Hidden stakeholders considered (assistants, informal influencers)
