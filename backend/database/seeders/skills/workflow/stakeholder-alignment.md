# Stakeholder Alignment

Navigate stakeholder interests toward consensus. Map positions, surface concerns, find common ground, and create actionable paths to alignment.

## What This Orchestrates

1. Stakeholder mapping (who matters, why)
2. Interest and concern analysis
3. Alignment gap identification
4. Disagreement resolution
5. Communication and action planning

## Process

### 1. Identify Stakeholders

Map the stakeholder landscape:

| Stakeholder  | Role/Function | Why They Matter      |
| ------------ | ------------- | -------------------- |
| [Name/Group] | [Their role]  | [Impact on decision] |

**Key questions:**

- Who can block this?
- Who must approve?
- Who will be affected?
- Who has relevant expertise?

### 2. Map Interests and Influence

For each stakeholder, assess:

**Interest:** What do they care about?

- Business outcomes (revenue, efficiency, risk)
- Personal factors (workload, visibility, autonomy)
- Team factors (headcount, scope, relationships)

**Influence:** How much power do they have?

- Decision authority (can approve/veto)
- Resource control (budget, people, systems)
- Political capital (relationships, credibility)

Create an interest/influence grid:

```
          High Influence
               |
    Manage     |     Partner
    Closely    |     Actively
               |
Low Interest --|-- High Interest
               |
    Monitor    |     Keep
    Only       |     Informed
               |
          Low Influence
```

### 3. Identify Alignment Status

For each stakeholder, determine current position:

| Status     | Meaning                    | Action Needed                    |
| ---------- | -------------------------- | -------------------------------- |
| Aligned    | Supports the approach      | Maintain, leverage as advocate   |
| Neutral    | No strong position         | Educate, address concerns early  |
| Misaligned | Has concerns or objections | Understand why, address directly |
| Unknown    | Position unclear           | Investigate before assuming      |

### 4. Surface Concerns

For misaligned or neutral stakeholders:

**Explicit concerns:** What they've stated

- "This will require resources we don't have"
- "I'm worried about timeline risk"

**Implicit concerns:** What they haven't said but likely feel

- Loss of control or influence
- Precedent-setting they disagree with
- Past experiences driving skepticism

**Questions to ask (directly or indirectly):**

- "What would need to be true for you to support this?"
- "What's your biggest worry?"
- "What am I missing from your perspective?"

### 5. Find Common Ground

Identify shared interests across stakeholders:

- What outcomes does everyone want?
- What risks does everyone want to avoid?
- What constraints does everyone accept?

Frame proposals around common ground:

- Lead with shared goals
- Acknowledge legitimate concerns
- Show how the approach serves multiple interests

### 6. Address Disagreements

For each significant disagreement:

**Understand the root:**

- Is it about facts? (Provide data)
- Is it about values? (Find higher-level alignment)
- Is it about approach? (Explore alternatives)
- Is it about timing? (Discuss sequencing)

**Resolution approaches:**

- Compromise: Meet in the middle
- Trade: Exchange concessions
- Expand: Find new value to distribute
- Sequence: Address concerns over time
- Escalate: Take to higher authority when stuck

### 7. Create Action Plan

For each stakeholder needing engagement:

| Stakeholder | Current Status | Target Status | Action            | Owner | By When |
| ----------- | -------------- | ------------- | ----------------- | ----- | ------- |
| [Name]      | Misaligned     | Aligned       | [Specific action] | [Who] | [Date]  |

**Action types:**

- 1:1 conversation to understand concerns
- Data/analysis to address objections
- Proposal modification to accommodate needs
- Coalition building (aligned stakeholders influence others)
- Escalation path if consensus fails

### 8. Track Progress

Maintain alignment status over time:

| Stakeholder | Initial    | Current | Trend     | Next Action       |
| ----------- | ---------- | ------- | --------- | ----------------- |
| [Name]      | Misaligned | Neutral | Improving | Follow-up meeting |

Update after each significant interaction.

## Adapts To

**Organizational complexity:**

- Simple (few stakeholders): Focus on direct conversations
- Complex (many stakeholders): Build coalitions, work through proxies
- Matrix (overlapping interests): Map carefully, address conflicts explicitly

**Political sensitivity:**

- Low stakes: Direct, transparent approach
- High stakes: More preparation, careful sequencing, backup plans

**Decision urgency:**

- Time pressure: Prioritize highest-influence stakeholders
- Longer timeline: Build broader consensus, invest in relationships

**Cultural context:**

- Consensus cultures: More inclusive process, patience
- Hierarchical cultures: Focus on key decision-makers
- Conflict-averse cultures: Create safe spaces for concerns

## Quality Checks

- [ ] All relevant stakeholders identified (not just obvious ones)
- [ ] Interests understood, not assumed
- [ ] Concerns surfaced explicitly, including uncomfortable ones
- [ ] Common ground identified and used to frame approach
- [ ] Each disagreement has a specific resolution plan
- [ ] Actions are specific, owned, and time-bound
- [ ] Plan accounts for stakeholder interdependencies
- [ ] Escalation path defined for unresolvable conflicts
